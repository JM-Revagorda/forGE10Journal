I’ve also reached out to a few more of my colleagues across the country, letting them know to get in touch if they hear of anything. Greetings, Operator! When we started the 20th Century Experience, we believed we were onto something, but even we couldn’t have predicted the overwhelming response from our visitors. It turns out a taste of life in the 20th century is not enough for many of our visitors, and after returning regularly, some are planning to live their entire lives here! Naturally, this includes the old idea of what was called “full-time employment.” In a happy coincidence, the introduction of “workers” to our park brings us one step closer to being the most authentic reproduction of the 20 century! Of course, now that we have a large number of people willing to assemble our food using their own two hands, we no longer require your services to design automated food assembly machines. Best of luck in your future endeavours! Thanks, The Management


Greetings, Operator! It’s exciting times for the 20th Century Experience! A few announcements to make regarding park operations: We are increasing *Mall* and *Skate Park* hours to 24 hours a day. All operators role-playing as Retail Associates at the Mall or as Cool Kids in the Skate Park will be assigned to one of three shifts. *Keytars* are now back in stock after last month’s supply problems. While we realise this is what happens when you hand machine manufacture your products, we have very strong feedback from our visitors that they love the authenticity. Due to increased demand, we have introduced more “jobs” for visitors to try in the *Industrial Zone*. Additionally, many visitors have told us they are curious to experience 20th century “burnout” and “existential despair,” so we are spinning up a new *Office Park* area to explore how we can bring these once-extinct sensations back to life. Thanks, The Management
