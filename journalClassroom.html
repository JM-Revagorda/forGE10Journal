Freshman Committee Orientation 8/16/2023

After our GE10 Subject Orientation had ended, several seniors went up the stage. They were the representatives of the committees that made up our college's Confederate Council of Committees. I watched the introduction of each member and committee as they produced some of the most interesting advertisements on what their respective committees do. Some of them have long descriptions on their powerpoints on what their committee can do, while others have their own unique style of portraying the responsibilities and roles of the committee in an unprofessional, though rather funny and relational, way. While others just say it in front of us, also telling the truth of what they do.

The entire program ran like a prepared show in front of us. As they told us the responsibilities of each committee, I noticed that each committee has different roles and inclinations. In the Aesthetics Committee, they help in producing some of the most elaborate designs for every event CCS has to offer. In the Transportation Committee, their aim is to check on what is needed by the other committees and to order and transport those goods or equipment to them or to the location where the event is held. While in the Administratice Committee, their responsibilities is to deliver messages or documents made by the Dean and her secretaries to other departments. Yet, even with all of this, they still need each other in order to be effective in what they need to do. Even if they have nothing to do, they try to help the other committees in what they are doing, such as Administrative Committee helping the Aesthetics Committee in making the props for an event, etc.

Although this doesnt seem much, this helped me appreciate more how they still need each other even if they are in different spectrums.

This activity helped me understand that college life is not only about studies, but also making the most out of life and to explore more on what you could do.

PEP Orientation 8/22/2023

This event was an introduction to the PEP classes that the PEP teachers will be holding after the Founder's Week. I was one of those who are sitting along the circle with those who have scheduled this activity.

This activity was eye-opening, as I have met for the first time, my college mates in the department. It was a fun experience. As I get to know each one of them, I have learnt the characteristics of each person I have met in the activity. I always believed that college was an introduction to a boring and unfriendly life, yet here I am, dancing around with the people who I will soon call "friends" and "colleagues".

This activity taught me that there is so much to learn about in life. It is not only to merely become successful in life, but also rather to enjoy what life has to offer, even if I lose.

Because of this activity, this had opened my eyes to the college life.

Balik Talent Lecture: “Syntaxes, Strategies, and Stories” 8/22/2023

This event was hosted by the College of Computer Studies featuring the speaker, Mr. Ernest Dale M. Acar, who owns a company that creates content for their client's FB pages and proves that programming, art, and stories can come together. I participated the lecture as a student, as I dont have any role in this lecture. However, I did answer some questions that he had during the lecture.

His lecture taught me many things. His lecture taught me that the right mindset can make anything possible. He also taught us that the right strategy such as a change in habits in order to accomplish more in the work that we do. And lastly, he taught us that we should enjoy the work, as it can help us gain a good mindset and a good strategy to overcome the difficulties that programming can happen.

I will use this teaching in my programming by taking breaks from time to time in order to start the work fresh. Then, I will utilize time-management techniques in order to accomplish my goal faster. But ultimately, I have to remember the joy of having a program or website to work, and the reason as to why I am here in the first place.

This lecture had helped me boost my motivation and my inspiration that art and coding can come together

NSTP Orientation 8/19/2023

The orientation was held in the Cimafranca Ballfield at 6 am in the morning by the NSTP-ROTC Organisers. We were still sleepy when we went to the grand stand, even hungry as most of us hadnt eaten our breakfast yet. Even with this, it was still necessary for us to be there. Before the morning rays of sun reached the grand stand, we had a Zumba performance given by the SU Athletics Department. Then the Morning Devotion came, until the Orientation for ROTC came.

What surprised me in the orientation that unlike the calm and collective marketing of the other NSTP groups, the ROTC were seemingly the most obnoxious, in a sense that it tries to make itself higher and mightier than the other NSTP groups. It threw me off, and even supported the sole reason why I dislike the military. Putting it aside, I did learn something in the orientation. To succeed in something, one must not only have the money, love, or teamwork to do it, but rather the right attitude in order to pull off a feat. Like how the LTS members care for the disabled, the CWTS deal in community projects, and the ROTC deal with on-ground tactical strategies, having the right mindset, attitude, and discipline can make the world go round as intended.

This orientation helped me learn that sometimes, discipline and compassion towards one's work can help one persevere through every problem that they may face. Things like continuing to find the solutions of a math problem, asking help from others who faced the same situation, and being brave enough to face the problem I have now are things like that. And because of this orientation, I have learnt to persevere through the right attitude.

All-University Convocation 8/6/2023

The All-University Convocation was held in the morning inside the Luce-Auditorium. It was about the Student Government of SU introducing to freshmen like me on what are the services that they have to offer, such as dorm housing, international affairs for foreign students, physical, spiritual, and wellness services, and even legal affairs whenever something bad happens to the student.

Unlike the other activities, where I dont normally expect anything, I was thinking that the convocation is just merely a very long boring lecture, just like the SUCCI meetings that I and my Dad had attended in Luce. However, I was caught in surprise that it was energetic and interesting, highlighted by the introductory acting done by some performing arts students on the various services that Silliman has to offer; it was like an ad that is placed during commercial break, but this time on the stage. During the main program, I was visited and appalled by the informative videos that they have made for this convocation. Although it seemed subpar compared to the ads that TV channels has to offer, it was interesting enough for me to understand fully what they can do for the students. For sure, some were just powerpoints and lecture, but the convocation had interested me enough to understand how it could go.

This activity emphasized to me that schools are a second home, in a sense that they deal with the lodging, with the loans, and even the legal activities that can help put disagreements at bay. Thus, this orientation had helped me understand the complexity and inner workings of Silliman's Student Government and helped me appreciate more on the various tasks that they have to deal with.

Sticker-Verse Sticker Making Orientation 8/11/2023

Held in 145 Cafe by Yu-Ha prints, this orientation teaches one on the process of making a sticker, the nuances of the different materials to be used to make the sticker, and the other things that one has to consider first before deciding to sell and make the stickers. I was the audience in this lecture.

I learned a lot of things from the lecture when it comes to sticker making. One is that it is a delecate process, as it requires some materials like a laminator, a sticker printer, a mat where you could cut the stickers, a sticker page, and others. More over, I learned on the various tips and tricks in order to be effective when cutting your stickers, like making an outer outline away from the main drawing so that you can have a guide when you cut them for selling.

Although this may be an expensive hobby to deal with, the cost cannot be compared when you have the heart and the want to make the stickers. Thus, the right attitude, material, and preparedness must come into mind when making these stickers. This activity helped me understand better on making stickers, and have taught me that if you have the heart(and the money) to do it, than nothing can stop you from showing to others your ideas.

PEP Class 8/25/2023

This is a subject that I was in that tries to introduce to us the concepts of mental health and spiritual awareness. It also acts as some sort of safe haven, away from the pressures that the other subjects gave us, even if it is minor.

Before this subject happened, I was thinking it would be just like the other subjects that we have. It would involve a pretty long lecture, an assignment, and a quiz. To my surprise, none of those happened! It felt like I was in a dream, most especially during their activities, as it is activity based. Moreover, everything is not graded! Being there felt surreal and refreshing, as it was not normal for me to be in such a relaxing environment. But, even with the subject being activity-based, it still taught us that our mental and spiritual health is more important than the work and assignments that we have to do.

This subject had taught me to take care of myself along the way. Things like treating oneself every now and then, enjoying the activities that one has with others, practicing one’s faith, and the like are some of these things that PEP had taught me. And because of this subject, I have learned to take care of myself every now and then.

Balik Talent Lecture: “Success: Its all about the mindset” 8/24/2023

This lecture was held by the CCS Department featuring the speaker, Ms. Louel S. Lariba, who was the systems analyst of a Singaporean company. Her lecture is all about how attitudes that one has developed in the classroom can be brought over to the workplace, and can contribute to the success or downfall of the person when working in the company. I was the audience there

This activity helped me understand that habits, particularly the soft skills like being a good listener, are very important to be developed when we are still in school as we can bring them to the workplace. More over, if we develop our good soft skills, the companies that we work in will trust us more. Even with the high amount of responsibilities that that comes with it. it still means that you are definitely a treasured employee of the business, and as such, will be able to go up the corporate ladder.

This lecture helped me understand that developing the right mindset will help me in the long run. Things like listening to the person, asking help from others who have encountered a certain problem that you are facing, being able to innovate and do more than what you could do, and others are some of the things that I could do to apply to the lecture.

Because of this activity, I have opened my eyes more to the importance of developing a good lifestyle and habit.
