All Saint's Day Family Reunion11/1/2023

During the small vacation week leading up to the All Soul's and All Saints Day, our family visited our relatives from Luzon in order to visit Dad's hometown, Gapan. After a long tenuos trip coming from Negros to Manila, from arriving in Baguio at 4 am and staying and enjoying the view with my nieces and my uncle, who provided us with his apartment, to going to Gapan early at 6 am in the morning and meeting my cousins finally for the first time in a long time in their house.

Although we finally arrived at the place and had a heartfelt reunion with them, we still had one more activity to do before leaving back to Dumaguete. We visited the graves of our grandfathers and grandmothers. It was the first time I ever met Dad's and my uncle's ancestors. Dad introduced to me those whose graves we were visiting to. Though I kinda forgot about it due to it being a little bit complex for me to remember, I still appreciated where my family came from.

Reflecting on this event, I realized that death is an inevitable part of life. Even if we try to run away from it, at some point later in life, we would be tired of running away from it and that the things that we have loved in life have soon disappeared. Death and decay is an extant of life. Yet, even with this, what matters most in one's life is not how one dies, but rather how he sets his teachings and his impacts to those around him.

After writing this reflection, I still missed my cousins and the time I spent with them. Sure, its quite short, it was a very meaningful experience for me. May I meet them once more before I leave the earth.

Family Rosary10/23/2023

I had organized my family’s evening rosary session. Although it was planned by our family to do this every night for the entire month to commemorate our Mother Mary’s month, we had found it hard due to the works that we had to deal with in our academe and in the workplace. Thankfully, as I organized our family’s session, everyone was ready for it.

As we went with the prayer, I realized how happy, beautiful and simple one’s life is when one is young and when one is free from worries and stresses of the future. I was even reminded that even if it seems our parents really hates us, the mystery of the finding of Jesus in the temple and the mystery of Mother Mary visiting her cousin Elizabeth constantly reminded us of how our parents loved us, and will still continue, even if we falter from the family.

We ended our session with a bid of goodnight to each other. In the course of the rosary, we hadn’t known that we were bonding over each other and forgetting about the responsibilities that we must do for our work to be finished. It acts as some sort of a stress free time; a time that we can be free and to remember the love that we gave. We went back to our work as I blew off the candle

Wake of my Mom's friend from Dipolog 10/22/2023

We went immediately to Dipolog after catching wind that her friend is having a wake. Catching the 6 AM trip to Dipolog, we traveled from Dumaguete to Dapitan port with the basic necessities. We didnt bring our car this time, unlike the other trips that we had like going to Cebu, to Dauin, to Bacolod, and vice versa. We were able to secure a ticket in the hot sunny afternoon of the same day.

We arrived at a new Dapitan City in the dead of night. After getting one of the fastest midnight rides I have ever seen in a pedicab going to Dipolog City, we arrived at a not-too-shabby inn. Even if it is midnight, thankfully a staff member was up in the lobby and helped us in our rooms. We slept in what one can call a box peacefully throughout the night. We woke up and prepared ourselves for the wake. We went to a nearby Mcdonalds to eat breakfast there, then roam around Dipolog proper to see what had changed when we were gone.

We went to the wake that her friend is in. Well, where her friend was. We went to the room where the wake was taking place and saw her, aged, sleeping peacefully in the coffin and ready to go back to the earth once more. Emotional, Mom was starting to tear up from all the memories she had with her. It has been a long time since they have been together, starting from when they were in high school. Her family was grieving along with her. A good friend has died.

Before she started working hard, Mom and her had met each other everytime she visits in Dumaguete. I couldnt imagine how it went, but it was surely beautiful to reunite again. Suddenly, Mom and her had started to fall out, due to the fact that Mom kept ignoring her requests to meet when she came to Dumaguete. This event was the last time they will ever meet.

As the angelic voices of the funeral choir sing along with all her friends and family that she had made during her time here, I realized that the friends I have made are very important. They shouldnt be ignored and shouldnt be pushed away, all because of some bureucratic and capitalist responsibilities. Instead, we must make space for them in our lives, even if we work. Who knows? It may be the last time that we ever see them and enjoy life with them again. It may very well be the last adventure that they have, and you arent there, you know?

Man, I miss my friends and the things I have and could've done with them. May I see them again in the future.

UCLEM Convocation 11/21/2023

This event happened in the afternoon at 5:00pm. Before the main event started, there was a hanging of the banners that we have made in the morning during our CHS times. However, the event was moved to the inside of the SU Church instead of the Ampitheater as hard rains had enveloped the campus area.

I was expecting a simple sermon similar to the ones like the Homily section in Catholic Masses and the sermons found in ordinary masses in Silliman's Church. What I got instead was an interesting, interactive, and relational sermon that taught us primarily on the wonders of God. Not only that, but we even started off with an introduction to the people around us. Though some of the members of the group I am in are familiar to me, there are others I have not seen yet.

During the event, I have learnt that unlike the other gods in other mythologies, our God is an entirely different being. He has no anger unlike the gods of Poseidon, Artemis, and others who punish those who oppose them, have insulted them, or had not done anything wrong to them. He loves humanity unconditionally, even if we keep failed him in every way possible. He even accepts us and cares for us, even if we keep on hurting him. And even on the criminals who we say that it is impossible to change their hearts, God still tries to help them and even accepts them if they had changed their hearts to goodness. Just like how a saint named John Bosco taught juvenile kids the way to the Bible, so can God do unto us even in the most sinful of people.

Because of this activity, I am inspired to help people to go back to the path of goodness, and to have hope for a better future.

Ordinary Mass 9/2/2023

This mass was held by the Mary Immaculate Parish Priests in the evening of 6:00 PM. Me and my family don’t normally go there for mass, but since we were busy the entire day and the only time we are free is late in the evening, we attended their mass.

Just like any other mass or event that I first attended, I dont think or expect too much on how it should play out. Their mass is just like any other masses that we have attended, the difference being the location. The mass was about putting our complete faith in Christ and in the Lord himself. Although there are times when we seek the help of someone, we shouldnt depend so much on the person, as there are cases where people can betray someone that they know or love. However, Jesus Christ isnt like that, as he never betrays anyone, most especially those who we view as wicked as God always gives them the chance to change themselves.

I will try to apply the message in my everyday life by remembering to pray to God, telling Him my fears, my happiness, and my sins. I also ask from Him guidance that He may be able to guide me through the challenges that I will be facing in the next day. Also, I will try to help others in their troubles and to become humble as well, as a way of repaying back what God has done for me and my family in my works and to acknowledge that God is definitely God.

This event helped me understand me more on God's everlasting grace. When one places his/her faith in Him, miracles can happen.
